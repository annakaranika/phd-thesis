\abstract{
The Internet of Things has instrumented billions of homes, buildings, farms, and factories
with smart devices, yet today's platforms fail to meet the needs of the people who rely on
them. Control logic is concentrated in vendor clouds and hubs that introduce latency, cost,
and single points of failure; users navigate a rich control spectrum with no structured
support; and fire-and-forget RPCs leave developers and occupants without
feedback on action progress or success. In this thesis, we argue that smart space
control must be treated as a first-class design target---resilient, observable, and built
around real user behavior.

Our work straddles distributed systems design and human-computer interaction, applying
methodologies from both and conducting multi-pronged evaluations. {\it We show how smart space
control can be made resilient and people-centric by: i)~decentralizing the
Sense-Trigger-Actuate control plane to eliminate hub bottlenecks and cloud dependency;
ii)~empirically characterizing how occupants navigate the spectrum from physical to
proxy-based control; and iii)~introducing a new control abstraction that exposes the full
action lifecycle to enable observable, programmable automations.}

Concretely, CoMesh is the first fully decentralized, hub-less STA control plane for large
commercial edge IoT mesh networks, using group-based coordination with zero-message
exchange and locality-sensitive hashing; formal proofs and experiments confirm
its scalability and fault-tolerance. Control in Context, a study of smart home
owners (survey and interviews), reveals that control is a fluid, situational
process shaped by reliability and cognitive load, rather than ecosystem boundaries. RASC
(Request-Ack-Start-Complete) exposes the full action lifecycle atop existing RPC-based
platforms; formal proofs and experiments confirm fast, low-overhead lifecycle state
detection and significant scheduling improvements over state-of-the-art baselines.
Together, these works advance the vision of autonomous, observable
smart spaces designed around the people who inhabit them.
}
